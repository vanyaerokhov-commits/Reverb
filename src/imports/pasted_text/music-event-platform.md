Introduction and Personal Motivation
My diploma project is dedicated to the development of a comprehensive digital platform aimed at simplifying the search, planning, and organization of attending musical events in the music industry. This topic is based on my long-term interest in the music sector, which began in childhood. I often attend concerts and various performances, paying special attention to sound design and the visual identity of groups. I believe that sound design and group identity are very important, as they attract people's attention. Additionally, I have experience working with a small group where we developed an identity.
I see the modern music industry as a dynamic field where innovative tools can improve the consumer experience, making it more convenient and integrated. The project combines app development with the creation of a brand identity, addressing real problems and allowing me to apply my skills in design and technology.
Problem Statement
Based on my knowledge, in the music industry, I decided to focus on the consumer base. I identified two main problems. First, the lack of a single digital tool that combines information about music tours and allows storing tickets purchased on different platforms. Second, discomfort when searching, planning, and organizing visits to musical events due to the fragmentation of services and venues.
Users are forced to use multiple applications and websites—such as Ticketmaster, Eventbrite, or Bandsintown—for tracking tours, purchasing tickets, and storing them. This leads to missed opportunities, confusion, and additional efforts, ultimately reducing fan engagement and planning efficiency.
Proposed Solution: Platform Concept
To eliminate these shortcomings, I propose developing (working title in progress)—a unified application serving as a hub for music tours and ticket management. This platform will become a bridge between fans and musical events, offering a simplified and user-friendly experience. It will be available in mobile versions (iOS and Android) and a browser version to ensure accessibility across different devices.
The application will function as a "script" or roadmap for the musical experience, aggregating information from various sources and providing personalized tools for users. Key features include:
Easy access to information about group tours: Search by artists, dates, cities with notifications about new tours.
Purchasing tickets directly in the app: Integration with partners for purchases.
Ability to plan routes: Tools for creating routes between events, integration with maps (e.g., Google Maps) for logistics.
Ticket storage: Import and storage of tickets from different platforms (Ticketmaster, Eventbrite, etc.) in one place, with support for digital wallet functions.
Personalized recommendations: Based on user preferences (genres, artists) and visit history.
Gamification elements: Badges for attended concerts, statistics (number of shows, unique artists) to increase engagement.
Ability to sell/exchange tickets within the app.

The platform will prioritize data security and convenience, with options for importing tickets via scanning or links. From a quantitative perspective, the project scope includes a full app prototype (wireframes, UI/UX designs, and functional demos), backend for notifications and integrations.
Research Components
To substantiate the project with market realities, I will conduct the following research:
Competitor Analysis:
Platforms for tracking tours, such as Songkick and Bandsintown, offer notifications about concerts and integration with ticket purchases, but do not focus on storing tickets from multiple sources.
Ticketing platforms like Ticketmaster and Eventbrite allow purchasing and storing tickets, but are limited to their own ecosystems and do not offer route planning or tour aggregation.
Universal wallet applications, such as Apple Wallet and Folio Wallet, store tickets from different sources, but do not integrate tour search or planning.
Specialized apps like TicketSwap allow importing tickets, but are oriented towards resale, not the full planning cycle.
Unlike them, (working title in progress) stands out as a neutral "all-in-one" hub, combining tour tracking, purchasing, storage, exchange, and planning with a focus on the musical experience.
Target Audience Analysis: The primary audience is music fans aged 18–35 who frequently attend concerts, with interests in genres like rock, pop, electronic music. This includes students, young professionals, and enthusiasts seeking convenience. Secondary users are event organizers for promotion. Insights will be obtained from surveys (e.g., through Reddit music communities) and industry reports on fan demographics, with an emphasis on pain points such as fragmentation and ticket loss.
Market and Feasibility Research: The music industry is growing, with an increase in tours post-pandemic (Live Nation reported $23.16 billion in 2024). Feasibility will include technical assessments (API integration with Ticketmaster, Bandsintown) and user testing for validation.
This research will be documented in a separate section, including visuals such as competitor comparison tables and user personas.


Brand Identity and Advertising Elements
In addition to the application, the project includes a full brand identity to promote the platform and ensure a coherent visual presence. This will be compiled in a Brandbook, describing:
Logos: Primary and secondary variations, including minimalist icons for app stores and full text logos for marketing.
Billboards and Posters: Conceptual designs for digital and physical ads, emphasizing themes like "Discover, Plan, Enjoy" to appeal to the music community.
Merchandise: Mockups for items like t-shirts, stickers, and keychains featuring brand motifs.
Colors and Typography: A palette inspired by musical aesthetics (e.g., bright neon for energy, dark tones for concert atmosphere) and fonts that balance dynamism with readability.
The advertising identity will be expanded to motion graphics for app demos and social media campaigns, ensuring the visual language aligns with the platform's innovative, community-oriented ethos.
